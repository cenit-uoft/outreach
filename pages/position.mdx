## Postdoctoral Fellow 

### Position Details

We are seeking international and domestic PhD graduates who are interested in performing cutting-edge research in the areas of computer networks and security. 
Successful candidates will focus on the design, implementations, and evaluation of communication protocols, network architectures,
and network security for the future Internet.
* Successful candidates must obtained their PhD in the last 5 years in computer science/engineering, or in a related field. 
Students who are in their final stage of their PhD studies are encouraged to apply.

### Funding

Funding will be available to successful applicants accoridng to their <a href="https://postdoc.sgs.utoronto.ca/current-postdocs/funding/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> admission pathways. </a>



### Expression of Interest

If you think CENIT is a right place for your academic adventures, we would like to hear from you.
We expect prospective candiadates to send us an email expressing their interest in conducting research at CENIT.
In your email, please include the followings:

1. A current CV
2. The name and email address of two academic references who have been engaged with you in a reseach project.
3. A two-page research statement describing:
   - Why you are interested in conducting research at CENIT, and,
   - Your past relevant experties in the CENIT's research areas.
* We will ask you for more information regarding your interest if we require additional information.
* You can learn more about our research by reviewing CENIT's <a href="/research" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> research thrusts </a> and
our <a href="/publication" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> publications </a>.

### Accepted applicants

Accepted applicants will receive an admisison offer from the <a href="https://postdoc.sgs.utoronto.ca/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> 
Postdoctoral office of the School of the Graduate Studies </a> at the University of Toronto and must meet the office requirements for 
postdoctoral fellowship at the University of Toronto.

## Full time Graduate Students

**CENIT is accepting applications for Fall (September) 2027.**


### What We Look For

We are seeking highly talented and motivated international and domestic PhD and Master students that are interested in conducting cutting-edge research in the 
areas of computer networks and security. Successful candidates will focus on the design, implementations, and evaluation of communication protocols, network architectures, and network security for the future Internet. 
 
### Funding

All accepted graduate students receive a guaranteed funding package as defined by the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at the University of Toronto.
* You can review the latest updates on the Electrical and Computer engineering department's <a href="https://www.ece.utoronto.ca/graduates/financial-support/package/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> funding packages </a>
before you apply.

### Expression of Interest

Before you submit your application to the Electrical and Computer Engineering department, you must email us and express your interest to study at CENIT.
We review your interest and get in touch with you. In your email, please include the followings:

1. A current CV
2. A cover letter stating your interests in CENIT's research directions and how you think CENIT can benefit you in your education and career pathways.
* We will ask you for more information regarding your interest if we require additional information.
* You can learn more about our research by reviewing CENIT's <a href="/research" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> research thrusts </a> and
our <a href="/publication" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> publications </a>.

### Final Application Submission

Successful applicants who have thier interests approved by CENIT must submit their 
<a href="https://www.ece.utoronto.ca/graduates/#apply" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> final application </a>
to the department of Electrical and Computer engineering at the University of Toronto.
* Please be mindful of the <a href="https://www.ece.utoronto.ca/graduates/#dates" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> application deadlines </a>
and get in touch early on to avoid missing the deadlines.
* Successful applicants must meet all the requirements set by the school of graduate studies and the department of Electrical and Computer engineering to receive an admission.

  ## Administrative Assistant (Term) - 1 Position

  We are hiring an administrative assistant to help with the efficient daily operation of CENIT.

### Your Responsibilites include:
  - Reviewing and assessing requirements needed to meet project timelines
  - Coordinating meeting schedules, agendas, materials, action and follow-up items
  - Handling sensitive and/or confidential information
  - Maintaining senior staff calendars
  - Scheduling travel plans and requesting appropriate accommodations
  - Drafting presentation materials
  - Building and strengthening relationships with stakeholders and partners of strategic importance
  - Conceptualizing, organizing and executing event activities

### Essential Qualifications
  - Bachelor's Degree or acceptable combination of equivalent experience.
  - Minimum five years recent and related administrative experience in an autonomous, decision making position, preferably in a University environment.
  - Experience supporting faculty research, research centres and groups.
  - Experience drafting correspondence, preparing briefing packages, applying and working with policies and procedures and organizing and coordinating special events.
  - Demonstrated experience in liaising with senior administrators, responding to and handling matters of a complex, confidential and sensitive nature.
  - Experience with organizing complex meeting schedules.
  - Understanding of the University’s mission and intersection with the broader community.
  - Advanced computer skills in Microsoft Office (must be able to set up templates and macros in Word and Excel, set up Excel spreadsheets and graphics, and prepare sophisticated PowerPoint presentations), and scheduling software.
  - Demonstrated familiarity with Zoom, Microsoft SharePoint and Teams.
  - Demonstrated analytical and problem-solving skills.
  - Demonstrated superior writing and editing skills.
  - Superior communications skills, both written and verbal (e.g., drafting written correspondence, minutes, proposals).
  - Excellent organizational and time-management skills. Ability to set priorities, manage multiple tasks and projects and meet deadlines.
  - Proven track record of using good judgment in dealing with issues in a fast-paced, dynamic environment. Excellent interpersonal skills.
  - Ability to work collaboratively and positively in a team setting, working effectively with a wide range of people and personalities (members of the University and corporate communities, students, etc.).
  - Thoroughness and careful attention to detail. Tact, discretion, sensitivity to confidential matters.

### How to Apply and Additional information
Please see <a href="https://jobs.utoronto.ca/job/Toronto-Administrative-Assistant-%28Term%29-ON/604256617/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-bold text-[#35538E] hover:underline"> the complete job advertisement </a>
on the University of Toronto website for more information and to apply for the job.
Applications must be submitted directly through the university's job posting.
